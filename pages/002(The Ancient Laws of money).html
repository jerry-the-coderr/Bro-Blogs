The Ancient Laws of Money

Saturday, 14 July 2025

Money is something most of us struggle with — whether it’s earning it, saving it, or just understanding how it works. But have you ever wondered why some people seem to attract wealth so easily, even if they started out with almost nothing? It feels unfair sometimes, but if you look closely, you’ll notice patterns. There are certain principles — or what I like to call the Ancient Laws of Money — that have helped people and even entire nations rise from poverty to riches throughout history.

Money itself has changed forms many times over thousands of years. From seashells to silver coins to digital payments, it’s never really stayed the same. But what hasn’t changed are the basic laws about how money behaves and how people who follow these laws tend to do better. Let’s dive into some of these timeless money rules that even ancient civilizations used to become wealthy.

Law #1: Spend Less Than You Earn
This might sound like the most obvious thing ever, but believe it or not, most people don’t follow it. Back in ancient Babylon — one of the richest cities in history — merchants and workers were taught to always save at least 10% of what they earned. No matter how little they made, they would first pay themselves. Today, people fall into the trap of spending everything they get. The first rule of building wealth is simple: spend less than you earn and save the difference.

Law #2: Make Money Work for You
Ancient traders knew the importance of investing. They would lend money or invest in trade caravans and get back extra gold when the goods sold at other cities. Even if you’re a student now, the lesson is this: don’t let your money sit idle. Whether it’s investing in knowledge, skills, or small ventures — your money (and time) should always be working to grow itself.

Law #3: Protect Your Wealth
In history, even the richest kings and merchants could lose everything overnight if they weren’t careful. They didn’t gamble recklessly or trust shady deals. They believed in protecting what they owned. In modern times, this means avoiding scams, bad loans, or unnecessary expenses. Be smart about where you keep your money and how you use it.

Law #4: Value Knowledge Over Money
Here’s something many people miss — the richest civilizations weren’t just rich in gold, but rich in wisdom. Ancient scholars believed that knowledge attracts wealth. The more you learn, the more opportunities you unlock. Investing in books, skills, and new experiences pays back way more than money kept locked in a box. Remember, a wise mind creates wealth.

So the next time you wonder why some people seem to have more money, know that it’s rarely luck. It’s because they follow certain timeless rules, consciously or not. You don’t have to be born rich to become financially strong. You just need to understand and follow the ancient laws of money — starting today.
